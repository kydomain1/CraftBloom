Hidden Gems: 7 Underrated European Destinations for Summer 2025

Alexandra Martinez June 12, 2025 9 min read

While Paris, Rome, and Barcelona continue to captivate millions of tourists, Europe harbors countless lesser-known destinations that offer authentic experiences, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural heritage—without the overwhelming crowds. As we move into summer 2025, savvy travelers are seeking these hidden gems that provide more immersive, sustainable, and memorable journeys. Here are seven underrated European destinations that deserve a spot on your travel list.

1. Kotor, Montenegro: The Adriatic's Best-Kept Secret

Nestled between dramatic limestone cliffs and the serene Bay of Kotor, this UNESCO World Heritage site combines Venetian architecture, medieval charm, and breathtaking natural beauty. The fortified old town features narrow cobblestone streets, ancient churches, and charming piazzas that rival any Italian coastal town—but with a fraction of the tourists and prices.

Must-do: Climb the 1,350 steps to the Castle of San Giovanni for panoramic views that will take your breath away (figuratively and literally). Visit in early June before peak season for ideal weather and fewer crowds.

2. Porto, Portugal: Wine, History, and Riverside Magic

While Lisbon garners most of Portugal's attention, Porto offers an equally captivating experience with its own distinct personality. This UNESCO-listed city cascades down hillsides to the Douro River, where colorful rabelo boats bob alongside centuries-old port wine cellars. The combination of azulejo-covered buildings, innovative restaurants, and welcoming locals creates an unforgettable atmosphere.

Where to stay: The Ribeira district puts you in the heart of the action. Book a boutique hotel with river views and explore the excellent port wine lodges in Vila Nova de Gaia just across the bridge.

3. Ljubljana, Slovenia: Europe's Greenest Capital

Slovenia's charming capital punches well above its weight. Car-free city center, a fairy-tale castle perched on a hill, and Art Nouveau architecture create a magical setting. As the European Green Capital, Ljubljana demonstrates how urban spaces can harmonize with nature—the Ljubljanica River flows through the heart of the city, lined with cafes and willow trees.

Perfect for: Foodies and culture lovers. The Central Market bursts with local produce, and the nearby Lake Bled makes an easy day trip that looks like something from a postcard.

4. Ronda, Spain: Dramatic Cliffs and Timeless Beauty

Perched atop a 390-foot gorge in Andalusia, Ronda offers one of Spain's most spectacular settings. The 18th-century Puente Nuevo bridge spans the dramatic El Tajo gorge, connecting the historic Moorish old town with the newer district. Beyond the famous bridge, Ronda reveals its layers: ancient Arab baths, Spain's oldest bullring, and whitewashed buildings that glow in the golden afternoon light.

Insider tip: Visit in late spring when wildflowers blanket the surrounding countryside, and enjoy wine tasting at local bodegas that produce excellent reds from altitude-grown grapes.

5. Tallinn, Estonia: Medieval Meets Digital

Estonia's capital presents a fascinating juxtaposition: an impeccably preserved medieval old town serving as the backdrop for one of Europe's most digitally advanced societies. Wander cobblestone streets past Gothic spires and merchant houses, then discover cutting-edge restaurants, hip cafes, and a thriving creative scene.

6. Ghent, Belgium: Bruges Without the Crowds

Often overlooked in favor of its more famous neighbor Bruges, Ghent delivers all the medieval charm with a vibrant university town energy. The Graslei waterfront showcases stunning guild houses, while Gravensteen Castle transports you to the Middle Ages. What sets Ghent apart is its authentic, lived-in feel—this isn't a museum city but a thriving cultural hub.

Don't miss: The Ghent Altarpiece in St. Bavo's Cathedral, considered one of the world's most important artworks, and the vegetarian-friendly food scene (Ghent has declared Thursdays meat-free).

7. The Azores, Portugal: Atlantic Paradise

This archipelago of nine volcanic islands sits in the middle of the Atlantic, offering dramatic landscapes that rival Iceland but with warmer weather and fewer tourists. Crater lakes, hot springs, whale watching, and lush green valleys create an outdoor enthusiast's paradise. São Miguel, the largest island, serves as an ideal base for exploring.

Adventure awaits: Hike to volcanic calderas, swim in thermal pools, kayak along coastal cliffs, and spot sperm whales in their natural habitat. The Azores represent sustainable tourism at its best, with strict environmental protections preserving the islands' pristine beauty.

Practical Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

June and September offer sweet spots—pleasant weather, lower prices than July-August, and manageable crowds. Many destinations also host cultural festivals during these shoulder months.

Getting Around

Budget airlines like Ryanair and Wizz Air connect many underrated destinations affordably. Consider renting a car for countryside exploration, especially in Portugal and Spain where public transport can be limited in rural areas.

Sustainable Travel

Choose locally-owned accommodations, eat at family-run restaurants, and prioritize walking or public transport. These smaller destinations benefit greatly from tourism revenue while remaining more vulnerable to overtourism—travel responsibly.

Final Thoughts

These hidden gems offer something increasingly rare in European travel: the opportunity to discover, explore, and connect without battling crowds or breaking the bank. As overtourism pressures major cities, these destinations provide authentic experiences that remind us why we fell in love with European travel in the first place. This summer, venture off the beaten path—you'll be rewarded with memories, stories, and perhaps most importantly, the sense of having discovered something truly special.
